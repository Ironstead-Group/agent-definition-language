# ISO/IEC (e.g., JTC 1/SC 42)

## Overview

- **Organization:** ISO / IEC — Joint Technical Committee 1 (JTC 1), Subcommittee 42 (SC 42 — Artificial Intelligence).
- **Relevance to ADL:** International standards for AI systems; useful for procurement, regulation, and global interoperability. SC 42 develops vocabulary, use cases, and technical standards for AI.

## Links

- [ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42](https://www.iso.org/committee/6794475.html)

- [PAS (Publicly Available Specification) and Fast Track](https://www.iso.org/publicly-available-standards.html) — options for bringing in externally developed specs.

## Submission Requirements (Summary)

- **Process:** New Work Item Proposal (NWIP); Working Draft (WD); Committee Draft (CD); Draft International Standard (DIS); Final Draft International Standard (FDIS); International Standard (IS).
- **Liaison:** National body (e.g., ANSI, BSI) or A-liaison from another body (e.g., IEEE, W3C). Participation often through national mirror committees.
- **Copyright:** ISO/IEC; contributor agreements per national body and ISO/IEC IPR policy.
- **Timeline:** Typically multi-year from NWIP to IS; PAS or Fast Track can shorten if based on stable external spec (e.g., ADL).

## Notes

- SC 42 already has standards on AI concepts, use cases, and risk; ADL could fit as a “description of AI agent capabilities and interfaces.”
- Consider PAS (Publicly Available Specification) to submit existing ADL spec as basis for an ISO/IEC standard.
- Liaison with Linux Foundation or IETF (e.g., via W3C, IEEE) can help with visibility and alignment.
